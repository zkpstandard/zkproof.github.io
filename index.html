Zero Knowledge Proof

Zero Knowledge Proof is a cryptographic protocol by which a party (the prover) can prove, without revealing any underlying information, to another party (the verifier) that a certain statement is true.

This breakthrough technology enables industries and individuals in many ways to go beyond the trade-off between data privacy and integrity.

Zkproof.org is an open consortium of industry and academia to standardize the use of zero knowledge proof.
